Functional Analysis

Fernando de Vasconcelos
January 2022

Abstract

Functional Analysis is the branch of mathematics which studies infinite dimensional vector spaces. This field have important results which affects other branchs such as Signal Processing, Control Theory, Partial Differential Equations, Quantum Mechanics and the list goes on...

In Linear Algebra we study several types of Vector Spaces, but we are focus only in the finite dimensional case. We may see during a good Linear Algebra course some examples of Infinite dimensional spaces, but they are not really the main object of study.

The differences from the finite dimensional to infinite dimensional are quite significant and that is the reason it is separated in two different courses. We will see that many results that holds in both finite dimensional and infinite dimensional cases, but there are crucial results that differs and we may see the consequences from those differences.

We start the theory recalling the definition of Vector Spaces:

A vector space is a non-empty set $V$ endowed with two operations $ +: V \times V \to V $ and $ \cdot: \mathbb{F} \times V \to V $, satisfying the following axioms:
  1. $ +: V \times V \to V $ is commutative, that is, for $u,v \in V$, $u + v = v + u$.
  2. $ +: V \times V \to V $ is associative, that is, for $u,v,w \in V$, $(u + v) + w = u + (v + w)$.
  3. $ \cdot: \mathbb{F} \times V \to V $ is distributive, that is, for $\alpha, \beta \in \mathbb{F}$ and $u \in V$, $(\alpha + \beta) \cdot u = \alpha \cdot u \beta \cdot u$.
  4. $ \cdot: \mathbb{F} \times V \to V $ is associative, that is, for $\alpha, \beta, \gamma \in \mathbb{F}$ and $u \in V$, $(\alpha \beta) \gamma \cdot u = \alpha (\beta \gamma) \cdot u$.
  5. There is an element $e \in V$ such that $\forall u \in V$, $u + e = u$.
  6. $\forall u \in V$, $1 \cdot u = u$.
  7. $\forall u \in V$, $\exists v \in V$ such that $u + v = e$

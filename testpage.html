Bhaskara Formula

Fernando de Vasconcelos
January 2022

Abstract

The Bhaskara formula allow us to find the roots of a second degree polynomial.

Let us proof our first theorem: The bhaskara formula:

Let $p(x) = ax^2 + bx + c$ be a degree two polynomial, then its roots are given by the formula: $$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$$
The key idea is to make several algebraic manipulations to transform the right hand side into a perfect square: $$ ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \Leftrightarrow $$ $$ \Leftrightarrow 4a^2x^2 + 4abx + 4ac = 0 $$ $$ \Leftrightarrow 4a^2x^2 + 4abx + b^2 = b^2 - 4ac$$ $$ \Leftrightarrow (2ax + b)^2 = b^2 - 4ac$$ $$ \Leftrightarrow (2ax + b) = \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}$$ $$ \Leftrightarrow x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$$
